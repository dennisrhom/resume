NOVEMBER 2024

Mission Design (W.A.L.L.E.)

Designed a conceptual lunar lander mission to test plant growth in two specialized chambers under lunar gravity. Focuses on first-order design and analysis of the Thermal Control System (TCS) and structural subsystem, with updated mass and energy balance modeling. Uses heritage from Chandrayaan-3 and IM-1 missions, the spacecraft is launched on a Falcon 9, performs a TLI burn, and lands in the Sea of Tranquility for a 14-day solar-powered operation. Part of ASE166M Curriculum

MAY 2024

Efficiency of Propeller Twist

This project investigates the impact of propeller twist on propulsive efficiency using a brushless DC motor and strain gages to measure thrust and electrical power consumption. The experiment compares two propellers with different twist angles, analyzing their performance and efficiency. The results suggest that propellers with less twist can achieve a higher thrust-to-power ratio. The study also highlights methods for measuring motor RPM using a photodiode and laser pointer

MAY 2023

Dragonfly - Aerodynamic Testing of Sci-Fi Aircraft

A low-speed aerodynamic study of a scale model of the "Dragonfly" aircraft from Rendezvous with Rama. Using wind tunnel testing, the team evaluated lift and drag characteristics at various Reynolds numbers and angles of attack. Flow visualization (smoke wands, tufts) and force measurement techniques were used to analyze aerodynamic behavior. Results showed performance limitations in Earth's conditions, but indicated potential viability under the higher Reynolds numbers hypothesized for the fictional planet Rama.
