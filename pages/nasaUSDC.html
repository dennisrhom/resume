NASA USDC-9: Microgravity Fluids

CADFluid DynamicsSystems EngMicrogravity

Overview

Part of the NASA University Student Design Challenge, this project focused on engineering and testing a passive fluid delivery system for microgravity environments. The goal was to analyze capillary flow through various porous substrates.

My role involved leading design verification and managing requirements presentation to NASA SMEs.

Key Contributions & Technical Approach

  • Coordinated a cross-functional team from concept to final design, ensuring all requirements were met and clearly documented.
  • Led preliminary and critical design reviews, presenting methodologies and findings to faculty and NASA experts.
  • Analyzed capillary flow through porous media to design passive fluid transport methods suitable for microgravity.
  • Iteratively designed 3+ 3D CAD models using ISS heritage sensors to test and document microgravity fluid interaction through substrates.
  • Documented fluid movement and absorption rates across different material configurations.
