NASA USDC-9: Microgravity Fluids

CAD Modeling Fluid Dynamics Systems Engineering Requirement Verification

The Challenge

In microgravity, fluid behavior is dominated by surface tension rather than gravity. This makes traditional fluid delivery systems (like those used in standard terrestrial plants or medical devices) ineffective.

As part of the NASA University Student Design Challenge, our team was tasked with designing a passive fluid delivery system capable of moving liquids through porous substrates without the need for pumps or electrical power.

Technical Approach

Analytical Focus

Modeled capillary flow rates using the Lucas-Washburn equation to predict how quickly different fluids would saturate specific porous media.

Software Used

SolidWorks for CAD, MATLAB for flow analysis, and LabVIEW for sensor data acquisition during ground testing.

  • Led a cross-functional team through Preliminary (PDR) and Critical Design Reviews (CDR) with NASA Subject Matter Experts.
  • Iteratively designed 3+ CAD models to interface with ISS heritage sensors for data consistency.
  • Documented absorption rates across 12 different material configurations to find the optimal substrate.
